Glioma stem cells in culture show interpretable spatial organizational domains with biological covariance

Date : April 2024 Author : Shamini Ayyadhury

Title : Glioma stem cells in culture show interpretable spatial organizational domains with biological covariance

Abstract : Glioma stem-like cells are useful models for understanding glioblastoma pathogenesis and in therapeutic screening and design. In culture, these cells maintain intracellular signaling networks and come together to form spatial structures that share morphological aspects with their original in vivo tissue structures. To explore this, we use image analysis algorithms to extract rich morphological information from cell culture images and show that these cell organization patterns span a spectrum that is robustly correlated with a developmental-injury response transcriptional gradient characteristic of GSCs. This relationship supports the biological relevance of 2D cell culture models and suggests how image-based therapeutic screening of GSCs and other cell lines can be improved by using cellular organization measures as a rich phenotypic readout.
